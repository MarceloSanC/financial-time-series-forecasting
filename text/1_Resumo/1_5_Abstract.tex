\begin{resumo}[Abstract]

This study investigates financial time series forecasting with emphasis on methodological robustness, uncertainty quantification, and experimental reproducibility. The research problem is associated with the difficulty of fairly comparing model configurations in out-of-sample settings, particularly when evaluation is limited to point metrics. To address this issue, an integrated pipeline was developed using market data and structured covariates, feature engineering, and Temporal Fusion Transformer models for multi-horizon point and quantile forecasting. The methodology adopts explicit temporal splits, temporal consistency controls, data quality criteria, and auditable artifact persistence using immutable persistence layers and a derived, reconstructible analytical layer. In the initial round, under a controlled cohort, model configurations showed very similar point performance. Quantile modeling provided extra information for risk analysis, but short-horizon coverage was below nominal coverage, indicating that predicted intervals were narrower than ideal at that horizon. Therefore, paired statistical analysis is still needed before claiming model dominance. The study concludes that combining quantile forecasting, strict temporal alignment, and execution traceability strengthens the academic defensibility of predictive evaluation.

\end{resumo}
